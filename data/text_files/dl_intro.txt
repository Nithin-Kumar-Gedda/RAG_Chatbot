Introduction of Deep Learning
Deep Learning is a specialized branch of Machine Learning that uses artificial neural networks with multiple hidden layers to learn complex patterns from massive amounts of data. It is inspired by the way the human brain processes information through interconnected neurons. In traditional machine learning, humans often need to manually select important features from the data, but in deep learning, the model automatically learns useful features and representations by itself during training. Deep learning models are built using layers of neurons where each layer extracts increasingly complex information from the input data. For example, in image recognition, the first layers may identify edges and shapes, intermediate layers detect objects or patterns, and deeper layers recognize complete objects such as faces, animals, or vehicles. 
Deep learning has become extremely powerful because of the availability of large datasets, high-performance GPUs, and advanced neural network architectures. It is widely used in modern AI applications such as speech recognition, computer vision, natural language processing, self-driving cars, medical diagnosis, recommendation systems, and Large Language Models like ChatGPT developed by OpenAI. Some important deep learning architectures include Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) for image processing, Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) for sequential and time-series data, and Transformers, which revolutionized NLP and modern AI systems. 
Training deep learning models involves techniques such as forward propagation, backpropagation, gradient descent, loss functions, and optimization algorithms that continuously update the network weights to reduce prediction errors. Popular frameworks used for deep learning include TensorFlow, PyTorch, and Keras. Although deep learning requires large computational resources and training data, it has achieved state-of-the-art performance in many fields and is considered one of the most important technologies driving modern Artificial Intelligence.
