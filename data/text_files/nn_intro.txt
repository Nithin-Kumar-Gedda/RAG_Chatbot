Introduction of Neural Networks
Neural Networks are a core concept in Machine Learning and Deep Learning that are inspired by the structure and functioning of the human brain. Just like the human brain contains billions of interconnected neurons that process information, artificial neural networks consist of layers of connected nodes called neurons that work together to learn patterns from data. A neural network typically contains three main layers: the input layer, hidden layers, and output layer. 
The input layer receives data such as images, text, or numbers, the hidden layers perform mathematical computations and extract important features, and the output layer produces the final prediction or result. During training, the network adjusts internal values called weights and biases using optimization algorithms like gradient descent to minimize prediction errors. Neural networks are powerful because they can learn highly complex relationships and nonlinear patterns that traditional machine learning algorithms often struggle with. 
They are widely used in applications such as image recognition, speech recognition, natural language processing, recommendation systems, autonomous vehicles, and medical analysis. Deep Learning is a subset of neural networks where models contain many hidden layers, allowing them to process extremely large and complex datasets. Popular neural network architectures include Feedforward Neural Networks, Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) for image processing, Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) for sequential data, and Transformers, which are the foundation of modern Large Language Models like OpenAI’s ChatGPT. 
Frameworks such as TensorFlow and PyTorch are commonly used to build and train neural networks efficiently. Overall, neural networks are one of the most important technologies in modern AI because they enable machines to learn from massive amounts of data and perform tasks that closely resemble human intelligence.
